What's New At OpenRemote - June 2013 Update
Last changed Sep 02, 2015 08:58 by Pierre Kil
Labels: rpi, rasberry, pi, razberry, zwave, isy99, insteon, knx, raspbian, hardfloat, softfloat, scene, schedule, rules, russound, tts, texttospeech, text2speech, turkish, locale, bug

The last few weeks have seen many community members come together and help each other with shared knowledge, tips, ideas, bug ﬁxes and new features.

One such example is the OpenRemote installation instructions for Raspberry Pi which is a very popular hardware platform with our community members. Jani Kahrama, Nejc Šmid, Sebastian Niepel, Igorius, Bob and Kal Ross have collectively given feedback and updated information on how to run OpenRemote on Raspberry. If you're curious about the differences between "hard-ﬂoats" and "soft-ﬂoats" and the Java performance improvements people are getting from the indevelopment Java 8 early access version, head straight to our Raspberry Pi installation page.

Another Raspberry related update is the contribution lead by long-term community member pz1 to integrate Razberry, a Z-wave enabled daughter card to Raspberry Pi. Razberry is now a third possibility with OpenRemote to integrate Z-wave-based devices.

marcello gallucci created a documentation page describing how to build scheduled scenes with OpenRemote. It is a nice example for those who want to get deep into rules programming and understand how to schedule actions based on conditions such as whether you are at home or away and have your automation system adjust its regularly scheduled actions based on changing conditions.

In the bug squashing squad, Kevin has been working on updates to the Universal Devices' ISY-99 bridge to Insteon powerline modems, a popular add-on for those who enjoy Insteon as their automation technology of choice. Slef, Kenneth Stridh and Marcus Redeker have brought in KNX updates while Torbjörn Österdahl has updated our integration to Russound sound systems and Simon Vincent updated our TCP and UDP protocols. Lawrie Griffiths is integrating text-to-speech functionality while Ali Haydar helped us tackle the infamous turkish locale bug (yes, Google it!).

Last night he got killed in a car accident together with his 3 small kids. This is a very sad loss to us all. Keep him in mind. He was a good guy.
