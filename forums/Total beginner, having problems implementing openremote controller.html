Posted by juha at Jun 01, 2013 11:37

EIB at least should be an outdated term by now, an older name for KNX.

DPT is a datapoint type – each device/actuator responds to commands and sends data that can be resolved based on the datapoint type. So for example DPT 1.001 is a switch, therefore the KNX packet with a single bit payload can be resolved to ON/OFF.

Other devices use different DPTs: sliders often in DPT 5.xxx family that return 8-bit values, etc.

Posted by aphaits at Jun 01, 2013 12:16

1/0/1 or 1/3/1 would be your group addresses – depends on the programming how many lights they respond to.

You can try it by creating two commands in OR designer:

protocol: KNX
command: ON
group address: 1/0/1 or 1/3/1
dpt: 1.001

and

protocol: KNX
command: OFF
group address: 1/0/1 or 1/3/1
dpt: 1.001

Then on the UI side drop two buttons on the design, bind one to your ON command and another to your OFF command, see which lights turn on or off.

Note that depending on your KNX/IP gateway, you may need to turn other applications using the same gateway off to establish a connection.

Posted by aphaits at Jun 01, 2013 12:48

There's a convention that splits the group address in three segments, where main is in range 0-31, middle is in range 0-7 and sub is in range 0-255

The largest group address therefore is '31/7/255'. Group address 0/0/0 is used for system broadcasts.

However, this is convention only, not mandated by KNX wire format although may be mandated by ETS (I don't know). There are other possible conventions that may have been used. So ultimately it is up to the programmer/installer to create these group addresses. They may have used some convention to make it easier to correlate group addresses to a physical location, for example, or they may have not. Depends on the installer.
