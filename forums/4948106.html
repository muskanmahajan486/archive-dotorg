Welll I've been doing a lot of reading but I've gotten to a point where I can't quite decide which transceiver to get.

I've narrowed it down to these four:

BC4+BCX-1 ---Bitwise Controls http://www.bitwisecontrols.com/products.php
IRTrans Ethernet http://www.irtrans.de/en/shop/lan.php
CommandIR II http://www.commandir.com/content/view/32/48/
USB-IR transceiver ---IguanaWorks http://iguanaworks.net/

The last one isn't really contender I think because it's not very featureful compared to the others.

Then again, despite being featureful the IRTrans device does have one big knock against it that this one doesn't.

It's built-in trasmitter only has one port....

This port can have up to 6 emitters but one can only output the same codeset at the same time via these emitters.

A second port can be added, but even then one cannot transmit two different code-sets from the two ports (via emitters) at the same time!

I'd love to hear peoples thoughts/experiences surrounding these devices and which you'd recommend and why!

Thanks heaps,

Jed

Comments:

Sharing some of my previous responses on the forums as well:

1) I'm not familiar with Bitwise Controls so can't say anything about that.

2) IRTrans is in use by some in the OR community already and as far as I've been able to gather the experience has been positive.

In addition we are discussing with IRTrans on how we can further develop our integration to provide even better experience with IRTrans products. So on the long run this may be a good choice if you're planning to use it with OpenRemote.

I've pinged them on your question about channels. Even if the same code is blasted on all channels this is generally not an issue since the IR codes tend to be distinct and ignored by devices that don't understand them. Depends a bit on the size of your deployment what the real impact is.

3) CommandIR II, obviously we've tested and it's part of our reference hardware setup with LIRC at the moment.

4) IguanaWorks, tested but had driver issues when using through LIRC. The ticket I created on this has now been closed and supposedly issue is fixed, however I haven't been able to test and confirm it.

I actually liked IguanaWorks a lot for sort of quick and dirty deployments to test IR integration due to its small form factor and low price. Where it may lack features I think it has its uses as well.
