The current implementation is very conservative and does close and rebuild the socket every time. Good point about it being a slow operation.

If you need to work around it, you could always do a copy/paste job of the TCP implementation and build a device specific protocol with your own connection management: Developer How To - Adding Your Own Protocol to OpenRemote Boss 2.0 Controller

Also, Richard Turner is extending the current TCP implementation to include some connection management that includes an option to leave a connection open but that's not quite ready for general distribution yet.
