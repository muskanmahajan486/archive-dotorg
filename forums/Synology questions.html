Comments:
  • Yes you can work on PC and move later to Synology. That is exaclty what I did.
  • A guy from the Synology community maintains an installation package for a number of boxes. On his forum is a user who confirms it runs fine on DS213+
  • The Synology OS sits on the drives you installed. Openremote is installed in a separate directory on these drives.
  • You do not literaly have to move your installation from PC to Synobox. Both can start with clean installs. You make your design in a cloud service. When you are finished you just update your PC and/or Synobox from there.
  • I am not aware of any shortcomings of the Synobox, but as OR is open, and supports may protocols, I can not tell for sure.

There are some minor installation differences depending on the CPU architecture of your Synology device. The x86 architectures are by far the easiest in terms of software installation as usually everything that worked on a standard PC (x86) hardware will work the same.

The ARM based Synology devices are fairly common though – these do require extra installation steps for a) the ARM based JVM from Oracle b) RXTX serial port library compiled for ARM architectures if you need access to serial port.

Other parts of the system should be cross-platform as long as you have the correct JVM installed.

For example, on ARM based Synology with Z-wave, you can check the documentation written by Joe Miller: OpenRemote 2.0 How To - Synology with Z-Wave
