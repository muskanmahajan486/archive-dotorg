Hi Mike,

I'm not sure what version of the WebConsole you are using but I would recommend a version from my server: -

http://multimation.co.uk:8080/controller/webconsole.war

The Web Console now includes the build revision along the bottom of the settings toolbar; a lot of enhancements have been made to the web console recently and security support being one of them.

Whether inside your LAN or not if you have security enabled on your controller the web console will need to login so I would double check that you have security enabled as you expect.

To access outside of your LAN you will need to do a couple of things: -

1). Open a port on your router and forward it to the PC in your LAN that is running the Tomcat server (e.g. open port 8080 TCP and forward it to 192.168.1.2:8080 - change IP Address to match your Tomcat server IP)

2). Unless you have a static WAN IP you will need to use a service like dyndns to give you a friendly URL to access your home remotely (something like http://miketirpak.dyndns.org, this is free and the key part is that your router supports dynamic IP services - within your router settings you specify the service you are using i.e. dyndns and your login credentials and whenever your WAN IP changes your router will notify dyndns so it can make sure that your URL always points to your home) If your router doesn't support dynamic IP services then you can use an app on your PC to do the updating for you.

There is also openremote.com (not sure if it is live yet) which offers a proxy service so you don't have to do any of the above, making life nice and simple but you have to pay a subscription. Using the commercial option means you just associate your controller with your designer account and then you just login on the console using your credentials anywhere in the world.
